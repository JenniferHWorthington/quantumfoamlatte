Fae & "Fuck..." - Monday, October, 6 2025

Reading has been a passion of mine for most of my life. I've had ups and downs with it. Right now I'm coming out of a down phase. With my mental health being, what can only be described as, a cluster fuck this past year, it's been a real struggle for me to enjoy reading. What makes it extra frustrating is I was working my way through a series I am really enjoying. The series A Court of Thorns and Roses was a fun read. Interesting characters, great world building, and who doesn't love reading Beauty & the Beast for the first time a second time! But seriously, I don't think I would have been able to get through anything else during that rough time. I struggled with focus, concentration, and experiencing joy. ACOTAR was captivating and had me always wanting to know what happens next, even though I had to put it down for, sometimes, months at a time. I was always able to pick up where I left off and just be immersed in the world and characters. Thank you Sarah J. Maas!

As already stated, I'm just coming out of a long-term funk when it comes to reading. I had to put down the fifth book A Court of Silver Flames. That was incredibly difficult for me to do. My tism thrives on completion of sets, so making the decision to try reading something else for a while was not easy, by any means. But so far, I'm pleasantly surprised by how shift has been doing my reading habits a world of good. I'm fighting the temptation to just jump back to Silver Flames. I know I need to let the palette cleanser do its job before I finish up ACOTAR. Of course, I just had to switch to reading something I'm just loving! But we'll come back to that.

I'm very selective in what I read, and re-readability is huge for me. I don't really read reviews on books or authors. I just kind of feel it out and something either grabs my attention or it doesn't. I have books on bookshelf I'm definitely going to read... just in due course. Sometimes I want a comfort book; something I've read over and over for its familiarity and because its like a blanky in word form. My tastes tend to stay in the realm of sci-fi, fantasy, romantacy (thank you Kelsey, Gab, and Janelle for that!), physics, self-help (cause I fucking need it!), and LGBTQ+ topics. That being said, I'm not limited to those topics. You're just more likely to find me reading something in that realm.

Alright! Back to my current read. I've started reading The Last Wish, from The Witcher series. I'm loving it! It's basically a collection of short stories (which makes it really manageable for me at the moment) that follow Geralt during a time before the events of the main series. I already was familiar with The Witcher from the video games, especially The Witcher 3: The Wild Hunt. Then I was really excited to find out Henry Cavill would be playing Geralt in the Netflix series. I'm disappointed he left; he WAS Geralt. I'm going to give Liam Hemsworth a fair shot though. Anyway, back to the books. I'm really looking forward to following Geralt's journey through the Continent and updating you all on what I think as I go through this series. I'm just so grateful that I'm able to sit down and read for more than 10 minutes at a time again.

With that, I leave you. Thank you for visiting the site and taking the time to read. If you want to stay in the loop when I post, please go to my contact page (A.K.A. Write Me a Note) and follow me on my socials. I try to post about anything that's new within the Foam. Toodles!
